OSHA compliance for oilfield sites

OSHA 29 CFR 1926.51 applies to all oilfield construction sites. The ratios:

  • 1–20 workers: 1 toilet
  • 20–199 workers: 1 toilet + 1 urinal per 40 workers
  • 200+ workers: 1 toilet + 1 urinal per 50 workers

For chemical-exposure sites (frac chemicals, solvents, lead-handling), OSHA 1926.51(f) requires hand wash stations with soap and running water — not just sanitizer. Our oilfield safety packages include the right ratios documented for your safety officer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Real answers from our oilfield desk. Call (833) 652-9344 for anything not covered.

How do you service remote sites without paved roads?+
Our oilfield service trucks are 4WD vacuum units rated for caliche, gravel, and unimproved access roads. We use lease maps to confirm route safety. Off-road service uplift ($50–$150/visit) applies only for deeply remote sites; standard oilfield road conditions are included in base pricing.
Service schedule for a drilling rig?+
Drilling rigs (24-hour rotation) typically need twice-weekly service per unit. Frac crews at peak operations may need daily service. We coordinate with the company-man or site safety officer and adjust as crew size changes through the well lifecycle.
OSHA compliant?+
Yes — standard units meet OSHA 29 CFR 1926.51. We supply the worker-to-toilet ratio chart with every quote. For sites with handwashing requirements (chemical handling), we pair standard units with hand wash stations meeting 1926.51(f).
Extreme heat or cold?+
Permian / Eagle Ford summer (110°F+): heat-resistant blue solution + increased servicing frequency. Bakken winter (−30°F): winter-grade deodorizer that won't freeze, drained hand wash water lines, antifreeze additives. Service trucks rated for both.
Typical contract length?+
Drilling: 30–90 days per well. Frac: 14–30 day windows. Long-term production sites (compressor stations, gas-processing): quarterly or annual contracts with consolidated invoicing. Volume pricing on contracts over 50 unit-months.
Documentation for company audits?+
Yes — service logs (timestamped, with driver photo on request), unit-count summaries by lease and well, OSHA-ratio compliance docs. Major operators receive consolidated quarterly reports across all active sites.
