Festival sanitation isn’t the same as event sanitation

A wedding caterer can plan around 200 guests with a luxury trailer. A festival operations director is planning around 25,000 guests, three days, alcohol service, multiple stages, ADA compliance, permit conditions, and the very real risk of a porta potty line that becomes a viral TikTok. The math, the logistics, and the failure modes are all different.

We dispatch festival fleets year-round — music weekends in Austin, Nashville, and Coachella-type desert events; county fairs in the Midwest; coastal marathons; multi-day food and wine festivals. The patterns repeat. This page documents how we plan, price, and service them.

Where to place units on your festival site

Placement is what separates a smooth festival from a viral porta-potty line. Three rules from our dispatch leads:

  • 1.
    Clusters of 8–12 units, not single rows. Clusters spread the lines and let you dedicate one cluster per zone (main stage, second stage, food court, camping).
  • 2.
    One ADA unit at every cluster, not concentrated in one location. Most permit offices will require this anyway, and it dramatically improves access.
  • 3.
    Hand wash stations at food vendors and bars — not at the porta potty cluster. Guests need to wash hands when food/drink is in their hand, not after they’ve walked back to the queue.
  • 4.
    200–300 feet from any stage. Close enough to be obvious; far enough that pump-truck servicing isn’t audible during sets.
  • 5.
    Pump-truck access path wide enough for a 30-foot truck on hard ground. Soft soil after rain becomes a serviceability problem fast — we’ll review your venue map before quoting.

Multi-day overnight servicing

For events that run multiple days, we run an overnight service window — typically 2:00 AM to 6:00 AM. Each unit gets:

  • Waste tank pumped to a residual
  • Bowl washed and disinfected
  • Paper and seat-cover dispensers refilled
  • Sanitizer dispenser refilled
  • Deodorizer puck replaced
  • Door, latch, and lock checked
  • Floor mat hosed

For festivals over 25,000 attendees we add a mid-day refresh window (typically 2–4 PM during a less-attended set) at the highest-traffic clusters. Hand wash stations get water-tank refills on a separate 4–6 hour cycle.

Permits and compliance

Most municipalities require sanitation documentation as part of the festival permit. We provide:

  • Total unit count + ADA-unit count tied to your projected attendance
  • Servicing schedule and waste-disposal plan
  • Certificate of Insurance ($2M general liability) listing your event entity
  • Driver licensing and OSHA-compliance certifications on request

If your permit office needs additional language, our ops team has worked with most major US municipal permit offices and can produce city-specific documentation packages.

Pair festival porta potties with these

  • Luxury restroom trailers for VIP / press / artist hospitality areas. Climate-controlled, flushing toilets — the difference between a press-room complaint and a press-room compliment.
  • ADA-compliant units at every cluster. Required by most permit offices and a real accessibility issue, not a checkbox.
  • Hand wash stations next to food vendors and bars. Health-department permits often require these explicitly.
  • Septic pumping & holding tanks for camping sections of multi-day events.

Frequently Asked Questions

Real answers from our festival ops team. Still got questions? Call (833) 652-9344.

How many porta potties does a festival need?+
Default: 1 unit per 100 attendees dry, 1 per 50 with alcohol. A 10K-attendee festival with a beer garden needs ~200 units. A 50K multi-day festival typically deploys 800–1,200 units with surge capacity at peak times near stages and food.
How often are festival porta potties serviced during the event?+
Multi-day festivals get overnight servicing (2–6 AM) plus a mid-day refresh at high-traffic clusters. Hand wash stations get water-tank refills every 4–6 hours.
How far in advance do I need to book?+
For festivals over 5,000 attendees, 60–90 days. We need lead time to stage trucks, schedule overnight servicing crews, and coordinate with permit offices. Last-minute orders may face partial availability.
Do festivals legally require ADA units?+
Yes — most municipal festival permits require ADA-compliant porta potties at 5% of total units (1 ADA per 20 standard) or 1 per 1,000 attendees, whichever is greater. We include this calculation automatically.
How do you handle waste removal?+
Pump trucks visit overnight on a routing schedule. Waste hauls to permitted sanitation facilities. Festivals over 25,000 attendees get two pre-staged pump trucks for surge capacity. Documentation provided to your permit office.
Can you bundle hand wash and ADA with the standard units?+
Yes — festival quotes are bundled. Hand wash near food/bars, ADA at every cluster, standard units distributed by zone. Single coordinated drop.
