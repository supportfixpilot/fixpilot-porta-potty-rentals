When hotels and resorts need portable restrooms

  • Guest-room renovations — A floor or wing under renovation closes 30+ guest-room bathrooms. Replacement capacity in service corridors keeps remaining guests comfortable.
  • Lobby / public-area renovations — Public restrooms offline during a 6–8 week refresh. Portable units in service corridors maintain guest experience.
  • Banquet hall overflow — A 1,500-guest gala in the grand ballroom outstrips 12 banquet-floor stalls. Luxury restroom trailers in the lobby loading area handle the surge.
  • Resort outdoor venues — Beach weddings, golf-course events, vineyard ceremonies far from main building restrooms. Luxury trailers staged at the venue.
  • Plumbing-failure surge — Burst pipe, sewer backup, or municipal water disruption knocks out guest-floor restrooms. Same-day delivery essential to avoid guest-relocation costs.
  • Pool deck and outdoor amenity events — Conference welcome receptions, corporate retreats, brand activations on pool decks where existing facilities are shared with daytime guests.

Hospitality-specific protocols

  • Discreet delivery — Service trucks arrive during low-traffic windows (typically 4–7 AM); minimally-marked or unmarked trucks where requested
  • Brand-aligned tier selection — Luxury restroom trailers (not standard porta potties) for any guest-facing placement at 4-star or 5-star properties
  • Service-corridor placement — Out of guest sight lines; coordinated with property operations
  • Attendant service available — For banquet overflow at upscale events; included with VIP-tier trailers
  • Pre-event walkthrough — For multi-day conference events and weddings; we’ll review placement with the on-property planner
  • Quiet servicing — Pump trucks scheduled around guest-rest hours and event programming
  • Custom branding wraps — Available on luxury trailers for property-specific branding or sponsor activations
