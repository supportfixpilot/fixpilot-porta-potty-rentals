Event Sanitation Planning: The Complete Checklist

By Jordan Reed, Senior Sanitation Operations Manager · Reviewed by Priya Patel · Updated 2026-06-11
March 8, 2026 10 min read

Successful events require more than just good food and entertainment. Adequate sanitation facilities keep guests happy, healthy, and coming back year after year.

Pre-Event Planning (4-6 Weeks Before)

  • ✓ Determine expected guest count
  • ✓ Calculate restroom requirements (1 per 50 guests base rate)
  • ✓ Check if ADA units are required
  • ✓ Assess whether hand-wash stations are needed
  • ✓ Verify venue has designated placement areas
  • ✓ Book your rental company

Factors That Affect Restroom Needs

Don't just go by guest count. These variables can significantly impact your sanitation requirements:

  • Event duration: Add 1 unit per 50 guests for every 2 hours over 4 hours
  • Alcohol service: Increase capacity by 25-30%
  • Outdoor heat: More hydration = more restroom visits
  • Age demographic: Events with elderly guests may need more accessible units
  • Food service: More food = more restroom usage
  • Evening events: Ensure adequate lighting to restrooms

Equipment Checklist by Event Type

Corporate Events

  • • Standard units (1 per 40-50 employees)
  • • Consider luxury trailers for executive events
  • • Hand-wash stations near food areas

Outdoor Festivals

  • • 1 unit per 100-150 festival-goers (high traffic)
  • • Multiple ADA units
  • • Hand-wash stations at food vendor areas
  • • Strategically placed throughout venue
  • • Consider permanent staff for maintenance

Concerts & Sporting Events

  • • Heavy capacity planning (1 per 100-125)
  • • VIP sections may need luxury options
  • • ADA compliance is mandatory
  • • Service schedule every 4 hours minimum

Placement Strategy

Where you place restrooms matters almost as much as how many you rent:

  • Place units within 200 feet of all activity areas
  • Create multiple stations instead of one large cluster
  • Position away from main photography areas
  • Ensure clear pathways for access and egress
  • Consider weather protection (canopies for rain/sun)
  • Place ADA units on stable, level ground

Day-Of Coordination

  • □ Confirm delivery time with rental company
  • □ Designate someone to direct unit placement
  • □ Verify units are stocked (toilet paper, sanitizer)
  • □ Check lighting is adequate for evening events
  • □ Schedule mid-event service if needed
  • □ Have emergency contact number on hand

Budget Considerations

Sanitation typically costs 5-10% of your total event budget. Cutting corners here often leads to negative reviews and reduced attendance next year. Factor in:

  • Base rental cost
  • Delivery and pickup fees
  • Servicing during multi-day events
  • Specialty units (ADA, luxury trailers)
  • Additional supplies (extra toilet paper, sanitizer)
  • Weather protection (canopies)

Event Sanitation Planning Checklist — Extended Guide

As any seasoned event coordinator can attest, a well-planned sanitation strategy is crucial to the success of any event. From festivals and concerts to weddings and corporate gatherings, providing clean and accessible restrooms is essential for ensuring the comfort and satisfaction of your guests. In this section, we'll share some pro tips from experienced event coordinators on how to handle common challenges and create a seamless sanitation experience for your attendees. Whether you're a seasoned pro or just starting out, these expert insights will help you take your event sanitation planning to the next level.

Here are some valuable tips from event coordinators who have been in the trenches:

  • Have a plan in place for late-arriving porta potty deliveries, including a backup vendor or a contingency plan for last-minute substitutions. This will help minimize disruptions and ensure that your event stays on track.
  • For alcohol-heavy events, consider ordering 20% more porta potty units than you think you'll need. This will help prevent long lines and reduce the risk of overcrowding and unpleasant conditions.
  • Designate a separate VIP area restroom for high-end events or events with VIP attendees. This will provide a more exclusive and luxurious experience for your most discerning guests.
  • Clearly label each porta potty unit with its location and any relevant instructions or information, such as "VIP Only" or "Handwashing Station." This will help guests quickly find what they need and reduce confusion.
  • Create a "game day" checklist for the event morning to ensure that all porta potty units are stocked, cleaned, and ready for use. This should include items like toilet paper, hand sanitizer, and a schedule for regular cleaning and maintenance.
