What Does Premium Restroom Trailer Service Include? (Tier by Tier)

By Jordan Reed· Reviewed by Priya Patel· Updated 2026-06-12

A “luxury restroom trailer” can mean a $300/day 2-station for a backyard wedding, a $1,200/day 8-station for a corporate gala, or a $2,500/day VIP suite for a celebrity event. The day rates are public — what’s included isn’t. Here’s a full tier-by-tier breakdown of what you actually get for your money, what costs extra, and what to ask before you book.

The four tiers at a glance

2-station tier — what’s included

  • Two private rooms, each with flushing porcelain toilet and sink with running water
  • Climate control — A/C and heat (not just a fan)
  • Hot & cold running water from a built-in fresh-water tank
  • Vanity mirrors and interior lighting
  • Soap, paper towels, toilet paper stocked at delivery
  • Optional ambient music from interior speakers (controllable from outside the trailer)
  • Setup and breakdown by our delivery crew — trailer leveled, water tanks filled, A/C running before you arrive
  • Same-day pickup in most metros

Footprint: 14′ long × 8′ wide. Needs ~12 feet of clearance for the towing rig to drop and set up. Standard 110V outlet (30-amp) for power.

4-station tier — what’s included (everything in 2-station, plus)

  • Four private rooms — typically 2 men’s side, 2 women’s side (separate entrances)
  • Premium finishes: granite or stone-look counters, hardwood-look flooring
  • Standard music system with bluetooth or aux input
  • Larger fresh-water tank (~75 gallons vs 40 gallons in the 2-station) — less likely to run out at long events
  • Exterior step lighting for evening events
  • Coat hooks and clutch shelves in each room

Footprint: 24′ long × 8′ wide. Same power requirement (30-amp 110V).

8-station tier — what’s included (everything in 4-station, plus)

  • Eight private rooms — 4 men’s, 4 women’s, separate sides
  • Two zones of climate control (one per side)
  • Larger fresh-water tank (~150 gallons) plus optional garden-hose hookup for unlimited water
  • Stereo sound system with subwoofer
  • Exterior LED lighting for evening visibility
  • Hand sanitizer stations at each entrance
  • ADA-equipped option — one or two of the rooms can be ADA configuration on request

Footprint: 32′ long × 8′ wide. Needs 30-amp or 50-amp circuit.

VIP / concierge tier — what’s included

  • Marble or quartz finishes, custom interior design
  • Premium fixtures: gooseneck faucets, Toto or comparable toilets, designer lighting
  • Zoned climate control across multiple suites
  • Concierge attendant included for the duration of the event — restocks supplies, keeps surfaces wiped, directs guests, addresses any issues quietly
  • Custom branding wraps on the exterior on request (your event logo, sponsor branding, wedding monogram)
  • Live event support from our ops team during the event — on-call dispatcher reachable directly if anything needs attention
  • Pre-event walkthrough with the venue, including dry-run of placement, power, water

Used at A-list productions, celebrity weddings, red-carpet events, and the kind of corporate events where the trailer is part of the brand experience. See our VIP trailer rental page for the full spec.

What costs extra (and what to ask before booking)

  • Generator — If your venue has no 30-amp outlet, you need a generator. Quiet inverter generators run $75–$150/day. Verify before booking.
  • Attendant service (non-VIP tiers) — $35–$60/hour. Recommended for events 200+ guests or anything where the trailer reflects on the host. Ask if it’s included or extra.
  • Multi-day rental — Friday-Saturday-Sunday weekend rates aren’t 3× the day rate but they’re typically 2.5×. Pickup on Monday morning is standard.
  • Wood platform for soft ground or steep slopes — $25–$50.
  • Distance-to-depot delivery uplift — Beyond ~25 miles from our nearest depot, expect a $50–$200 add-on.
  • Custom branding (VIP only) — $200–$800 depending on artwork complexity. Provide artwork 14+ days in advance.
  • Damage / cleaning beyond normal use — If the trailer gets vandalized, used as a holding area for muddy boots, or significantly soiled beyond standard use, you’ll see a cleaning fee. Normal use is included.

Three questions to ask any premium-trailer vendor

  1. “Is the trailer pre-cooled / pre-heated before guests arrive?” Some operators turn the A/C on at delivery. We turn it on 60–90 minutes before doors so the trailer is comfortable from the first guest.
  2. “What happens if the water tank runs out mid-event?” If the operator’s answer is “just don’t flush” or “we’ll send a truck,” that’s a problem. Ask about garden-hose hookup capacity for events over 4 hours / 250 guests.
  3. “Can I see the unit before booking?” A real operator with a fleet says yes (or sends recent photos). An aggregator passing through someone else’s fleet says “we’ll send a similar unit” — meaning you don’t actually know what’ll show up.
