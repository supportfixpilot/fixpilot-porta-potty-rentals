Average Porta Potty Rental Costs in 2026

The average cost to rent a porta potty in 2026 ranges from $75 to $500+ per unit depending on the type of unit, rental duration, and location. Here's a quick breakdown:

Deluxe Unit $100 - $175 $200 - $300 $450 - $650

Standard Porta Potty Rental Costs

Standard porta potties are the most affordable option and are perfect for:

  • Construction sites with fewer than 10 workers
  • Small outdoor events (under 100 attendees)
  • Weekend projects
  • Temporary work sites

2026 Average Standard Unit Costs:

  • 1-day rental: $75 - $125 per unit
  • Weekend (2 days): $100 - $175 per unit
  • Weekly: $150 - $225 per unit
  • Monthly: $350 - $500 per unit

Most rental companies include delivery, pickup, and one weekly servicing in their standard pricing.

Deluxe Porta Potty Rental Costs

Deluxe units include additional features like:

  • Flushing toilet
  • Hand sanitizer dispenser
  • Interior lighting
  • Larger interior space
  • Better ventilation

2026 Average Deluxe Unit Costs:

  • 1-day rental: $100 - $175 per unit
  • Weekend (2 days): $150 - $250 per unit
  • Weekly: $200 - $300 per unit
  • Monthly: $450 - $650 per unit

Luxury Restroom Trailer Costs

Luxury restroom trailers are ideal for upscale events and VIP experiences:

  • Climate-controlled (A/C and heating)
  • Flushing toilets with running water
  • Vanity sinks with mirrors
  • Premium lighting and décor
  • Sound system compatibility

2-Stall Trailers

  • Daily: $250 - $350
  • Weekly: $500 - $700

5+ Stall Trailers

  • Daily: $400 - $600

ADA-Compliant Unit Costs

ADA units are required by law for public events and construction sites. They feature:

  • Ramp access
  • Wider doorways (32"+)
  • Interior handrails
  • More floor space for wheelchairs
  • Lower toilet height

2026 Average ADA Unit Costs:

  • 1-day rental: $125 - $200 per unit
  • Weekly: $250 - $350 per unit
  • Monthly: $550 - $750 per unit

Factors That Affect Your Rental Cost

1. Location & Distance

Rural areas or locations far from the rental company may incur delivery fees of $25-$150+.

2. Season & Demand

Peak season (spring-fall) can see prices increase 20-40% due to high demand for outdoor events and construction.

3. Rental Duration

Longer rentals typically get better per-day rates. Monthly contracts often save 30-50% compared to daily rates.

4. Quantity Discounts

Renting 5+ units usually qualifies for bulk pricing, saving 10-25% per unit.

5. Additional Services

Extra servicing, luxury amenities, or same-day delivery can add $25-$100+ per service.
