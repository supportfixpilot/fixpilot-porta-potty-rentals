How Many Porta Potties Do I Need for My Wedding?

By Jordan Reed, Senior Sanitation Operations Manager · Reviewed by Priya Patel · Updated 2026-06-11
March 10, 2026 6 min read

Your wedding day is supposed to be perfect. Long lines for the restroom shouldn't be part of the memory. Here's how to calculate the right number of portable restrooms for your celebration.

The Basic Formula

For most wedding receptions, the general rule is 1 porta potty per 50 guests. However, several factors can increase this number:

  • If alcohol is being served, increase by 20-30%
  • For outdoor venues with no indoor restrooms, double the estimate
  • If your venue is in a remote location, consider additional units for convenience
  • Multi-day events (rehearsal dinner + wedding day) need more units

ADA-Accessible Units Are Essential

Federal law requires accessible restroom options at public events. For weddings, we recommend:

  • 1 ADA unit per 500 guests minimum
  • ADA units should be positioned near the main seating area
  • Consider renting an ADA unit even for smaller gatherings for elderly relatives

Luxury Trailers: Worth the Investment?

For upscale weddings, luxury restroom trailers are increasingly popular. They offer:

  • Climate control (air conditioning/heating)
  • Flushing toilets and running water sinks
  • Vanity mirrors and proper lighting
  • A more dignified experience for your guests
  • Positive reviews and photos guests will share

Additional Tips

  • Placement matters: Position units within 100 feet of the dance floor but not in direct view of photos
  • Lighting: Ensure pathways to restrooms are well-lit for evening events
  • Maintenance: For events over 4 hours, schedule a mid-event service
  • Privacy screens: Request these if units will be visible from the dance floor

Pro Tip

Place a small basket with tissues, hand sanitizer, and a few feminine products inside each unit. Your female guests will thank you!

How Many Porta Potties for a Wedding — Extended Guide

Case Study 1: Rustic Barn Wedding in Texas

In the heart of Texas, a lovely couple tied the knot at a rustic barn wedding with 100 guests. The venue was picturesque, with rolling hills and a charming barn that added to the rustic ambiance. The couple chose to serve alcohol, which increased the demand for porta potty units. Based on our calculations, we recommended 3-4 units for the event. The total cost range for the units was between $500-$700. However, the couple initially underestimated the number of units needed and only rented 2, which led to long lines and discomfort for their guests. They had to emergency rent an additional unit, adding to their overall cost.

Case Study 2: Outdoor Garden Wedding in California

In sunny California, a beautiful outdoor garden wedding took place with 200 guests. The couple opted for a luxurious garden setting, complete with lush greenery and a serene atmosphere. With alcohol being served, we recommended 5-6 porta potty units to ensure guest comfort. The total cost range for the units was between $1,200-$1,500. Unfortunately, the couple underestimated the number of units needed and only rented 4, which resulted in overcrowding and dissatisfaction among their guests. They had to scramble to find additional units, which were fortunately available, but at a higher cost. This experience taught them the importance of accurate porta potty planning.

Case Study 3: Upscale Tented Wedding in New York

In the Empire State, a grand upscale tented wedding took place with 400 guests. The couple spared no expense in creating an opulent atmosphere, complete with fine dining and premium alcohol. Given the large guest count and alcohol service, we recommended 10-12 porta potty units to maintain guest comfort and satisfaction. The total cost range for the units was between $3,000-$3,500. Initially, the couple considered renting only 8 units, but fortunately, they took our advice and rented the recommended number. This decision paid off, as their guests praised the ample and clean facilities. The couple learned that investing in sufficient porta potty units is crucial for a successful and stress-free wedding.
