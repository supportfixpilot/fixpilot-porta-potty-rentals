Las Vegas's Clark County construction market operates at a scale unique in North America. Allegiant Stadium's completion launched a wave of Las Vegas Boulevard South mixed-use development extending the resort corridor toward Mandalay Bay and Town Square Las Vegas. MSG Sphere — the world's largest spherical structure — is transforming the east end of the Las Vegas Strip near the Venetian Resort, triggering adjacent hotel and entertainment construction throughout the Paradise Township unincorporated area. The Las Vegas Convention Center's West Hall Phase II expansion, Resorts World Las Vegas's $4.3 billion opening, and the ongoing renovation cycle of legacy properties — Caesars Palace, Bellagio, MGM Grand — create continuous construction activity throughout Clark County. Henderson's commercial and industrial construction in the I-215 corridor, Boulder City's solar farm construction in the Eldorado Valley, and North Las Vegas's warehousing expansion adjacent to Nellis Air Force Base add volume to one of the most active construction sanitation markets in the Western United States.

Las Vegas's event market is driven by the convergence of sports, entertainment, and conventions that makes it the world's entertainment capital. Formula 1 Las Vegas Grand Prix along Las Vegas Boulevard — inaugurated in 2023 — brings 300,000 international visitors for race weekend, creating an unprecedented outdoor event sanitation challenge across a 3.86-mile street circuit through the resort corridor. NFL Pro Bowl, college football bowl games at Allegiant Stadium, and the Vegas Golden Knights Stanley Cup playoff games add sports event volume. CES at the Las Vegas Convention Center brings 180,000 tech industry professionals in January. EDC Las Vegas at Las Vegas Motor Speedway draws 170,000 attendees nightly over three days. Rock in Rio USA, Life is Beautiful Music Festival in the Arts District, and the Las Vegas Marathon complete a calendar where every weekend brings major events to Clark County.
