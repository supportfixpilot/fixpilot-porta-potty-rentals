Denver's Adams, Arapahoe, and Denver County construction market is defined by the I-70 Mountain Corridor expansion — a $1.2 billion project tunneling beneath the Elyria-Swansea neighborhood — and the National Western Center redevelopment of the 250-acre campus north of downtown. The Denver International Airport's South Terminal Redevelopment Program, Ball Arena's surrounding sports and entertainment district development in the Speer neighborhood, and the explosive growth of mixed-use projects along the I-25 corridor through the Baker and South Broadway neighborhoods keep Denver metro contractors active year-round. Front Range industrial construction in Commerce City and Henderson, residential development in Stapleton's Central Park neighborhood, and the tech office campus expansions in the Denver Tech Center at Arapahoe County keep Denver County's portable sanitation demand diverse and consistent. Denver's outdoor event culture thrives at high altitude. Great American Beer Festival at the Colorado Convention Center draws 60,000 craft beer enthusiasts each October. The Colfax Marathon routes through Capitol Hill and City Park, Denver PrideFest at Civic Center Park draws 500,000 attendees over the weekend, and A Taste of Colorado at Civic Center Park closes each Labor Day weekend with 500,000+ visitors. Outdoor concerts at Fiddler's Green Amphitheatre in Greenwood Village, Denver Broncos tailgates at Empower Field at Mile High, and the Cherry Creek Arts Festival in Cherry Creek North complete a summer calendar that makes Denver one of Colorado's most active event sanitation markets.
