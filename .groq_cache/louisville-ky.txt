Louisville's Jefferson County construction market is experiencing sustained growth driven by the Louisville-Jefferson County Metropolitan Sewer District's $850 million Waterway Protection Program — the largest infrastructure investment in Louisville's history — which is rebuilding combined sewer overflow systems throughout Butchertown, Clifton, and the Highlands neighborhoods. UofL Health's $1.5 billion hospital and medical complex expansion on the east end of downtown, NuLu's (New Louisville) continued mixed-use densification along East Market Street, and the Louisville Metro Housing Authority's Sheppard Square mixed-income redevelopment add institutional and residential construction to Jefferson County's pipeline. Ford Motor Company's BlueOval SK Battery Park in Glendale — a $5.8 billion joint venture with SK Innovation creating 5,000 jobs — is the largest single economic development project in Kentucky's history and has triggered supporting infrastructure construction throughout Hardin County and the I-65 corridor. Louisville's event calendar is anchored by the Kentucky Derby — the most famous two minutes in sports. The Kentucky Derby Festival, spanning the two weeks preceding the first Saturday in May, draws 1.5 million visitors to Louisville and Jefferson County with the Pegasus Parade through downtown Louisville, Thunder Over Louisville airshow and fireworks at Waterfront Park drawing 700,000 single-event spectators, and dozens of satellite events throughout the metro area. Louisville Slugger Field's Louisville Bats games, the Louisville Cardinal football at Cardinal Stadium, the PGA Championship at Valhalla Golf Club in eastern Jefferson County, and Louder Than Life Music Festival at Kentucky Exposition Center add consistent event sanitation demand throughout Jefferson County. Bardstown Road's restaurant district outdoor events, the Speed Art Museum outdoor programming, and Churchill Downs spring and fall race meets complete a calendar that makes Louisville one of Kentucky's most active portable sanitation markets.
