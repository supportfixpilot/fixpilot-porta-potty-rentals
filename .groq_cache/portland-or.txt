Portland's Multnomah County construction market is shaped by the state's strongest urban growth boundary policies in the nation — policies that concentrate construction intensity in a dense urban core while suburban expansion is legally constrained. The Pearl District's Phase II development along the NW Flanders Street corridor, the emerging Division Street mixed-use corridor in Division-Clinton, and the Lloyd District's transformation from mid-century office park to transit-oriented mixed-use anchor keep Portland contractors active within the urban growth boundary. The City of Portland's ADU amnesty program has triggered a secondary wave of backyard cottage construction throughout Northeast Portland, Sellwood, and the Hawthorne neighborhoods. Deschutes Brewery's Portland production facility expansion in the Northwest Industrial area, the Columbia Corridor's warehouse modernization program, and the Oregon Health & Science University's Marquam Hill campus expansion add commercial and institutional construction volume.
