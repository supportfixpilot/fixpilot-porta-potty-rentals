Phoenix's Maricopa County construction market is the fastest-growing major metro construction market in the United States. The Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company's $40 billion fab complex in North Phoenix — the largest foreign investment in American manufacturing history — has triggered an unprecedented semiconductor supply chain construction wave through the Deer Valley and Union Hills corridors. Intel's Chandler campus expansion, TSMC's supplier park construction in Glendale, and the downtown Phoenix Rio Salado redevelopment keep Maricopa County general contractors booked through 2027. Suburban residential construction in Buckeye, Queen Creek, and the Eastmark master-planned community in Mesa add volume to an already hyperactive market. Phoenix's extreme summer heat (115°F peak) creates unique portable sanitation challenges: crew comfort and OSHA compliance in heat above 110°F require more frequent servicing, shaded placement, and cold-water hand wash station pairing that construction sanitation vendors unfamiliar with the desert environment routinely underprovide.
